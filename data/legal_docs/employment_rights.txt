Employment Rights in India — Overview

The Payment of Wages Act and the Code on Wages, 2019 govern timely payment of wages to employees. Employers are generally required to pay wages within a defined period after the end of the wage cycle (commonly by the 7th or 10th of the following month, depending on establishment size).

Under the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, employees who complete 5 years of continuous service with an employer are generally entitled to gratuity upon leaving the organization, calculated based on last drawn salary and years of service.

The Maternity Benefit Act, 1961 (as amended in 2017) entitles eligible women employees to 26 weeks of paid maternity leave for the first two children.

Termination of employment is governed by the employment contract, applicable Standing Orders, and in some cases the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 for workman-category employees. Notice period requirements and severance conditions vary based on the nature of employment and applicable state rules.

The Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH) Act, 2013 mandates that organizations with 10 or more employees constitute an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) to address workplace harassment complaints.
