Consumer Rights in India — Overview

Under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, every consumer has six recognized rights: the right to safety, the right to be informed, the right to choose, the right to be heard, the right to seek redressal, and the right to consumer education.

If a consumer receives a defective product or deficient service, they can file a complaint with the District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission if the claim value is up to Rs. 1 crore. Complaints above this go to the State Commission, and above Rs. 10 crore to the National Commission.

A consumer complaint must generally be filed within two years from the date the cause of action arose. E-commerce platforms are now explicitly covered under the 2019 Act, including rules around misleading advertisements and unfair trade practices.

Consumers can also approach Consumer Mediation Cells for faster, out-of-court resolution before proceeding to formal litigation.
