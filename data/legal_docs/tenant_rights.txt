Tenant Rights in India — Overview

Rental agreements in India are primarily governed by state-specific Rent Control Acts, though the Model Tenancy Act, 2021 provides a modern framework many states are adopting. A written rental agreement is strongly recommended and should specify rent amount, security deposit, maintenance responsibilities, and notice period.

Security deposits are typically capped at two months' rent for residential properties and six months' for commercial properties under the Model Tenancy Act, though local practice varies significantly by city.

A landlord generally cannot evict a tenant without proper legal notice and due process through a Rent Court or Civil Court, except in cases explicitly defined in the rental agreement (such as non-payment of rent for a specified period). Arbitrary eviction without notice is not legally permitted in most states.

Tenants are entitled to essential services (water, electricity) and a landlord withholding these to force eviction is generally considered an illegal practice and can be challenged legally.

Disputes are typically first addressed through a Rent Authority (where established under the Model Tenancy Act) before escalating to civil courts.
